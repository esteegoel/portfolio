home about design rowing

my rowing journey began in july of 2014 (age 11) when i was signed up up for a "learn to row" camp. as a soccer player, i was completely uninterested. used to high-energy competitive sports, sitting in a boat and rowing down a river sounded completely boring. but after day one, i was hooked, and soon dropped soccer to spend the next 9 years obsessed with rowing.

rowing has certainly shaped who i am, and many of the key lesson have been carried into the designs i create.

it’s an unmatched feeling -- gliding down the river in a shell with oars bending and straightening as they cut the water, the synchronicity of it all. it’s painful, the hand blisters that pop and burst with every stroke, the extreme soreness combatted only by extravagant stretches and muscle rolling, the odd sequential twisting and positioning of your body parts. it’s demanding, long hours of work to shave a second off your 2k test, days of weightlifting to increase your power-to-weight ratio. it’s a bonding experience, seeing your boat-mates for three hours each day, sharing hotel rooms at regattas, going through the absolute best and worst of times together. it’s rowing, the sport i devote so much of my extracurricular time to, the sport that teaches me discipline, focus, organization, persistence, team work, and how to win or how to handle a loss. i don’t ever want to stop rowing.
